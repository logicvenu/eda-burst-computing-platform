# Executive Summary

## Overview

Electronic Design Automation (EDA) workloads used in semiconductor design require substantial compute resources during simulation, synthesis, timing analysis, verification, place-and-route, and tape-out activities.

Traditional on-premises High Performance Computing (HPC) clusters often face resource constraints during peak engineering cycles, resulting in increased queue wait times, delayed project milestones, and reduced engineering productivity.

The Cloud-Agnostic EDA Burst Computing Platform provides a hybrid architecture that enables organizations to seamlessly extend on-premises HPC environments into public cloud infrastructure, leveraging elastic compute capacity while preserving existing engineering workflows, storage systems, and software licensing models.

---

## Business Challenges

Semiconductor organizations commonly encounter the following challenges:

### Limited Compute Capacity

Engineering workloads often exceed available HPC resources during critical design and verification cycles.

### Long Infrastructure Procurement Cycles

Expanding on-premises infrastructure requires significant capital investment and long lead times.

### Resource Utilization Inefficiencies

Infrastructure is frequently underutilized outside peak demand periods.

### Increasing Design Complexity

Modern semiconductor designs require significantly greater simulation and verification capacity than previous generations.

### Cost Optimization

Organizations seek to balance performance requirements with operational and capital expenditures.
