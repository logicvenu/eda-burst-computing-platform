# Executive Summary

## Introduction

Electronic Design Automation (EDA) workloads require massive computational resources during simulation, synthesis, verification, place-and-route, timing analysis, and tape-out activities.

Traditional on-premises High Performance Computing (HPC) environments often struggle to meet peak demand requirements, resulting in longer engineering cycles and delayed product delivery.

The Cloud-Agnostic EDA Burst Computing Platform enables organizations to elastically extend on-premises HPC environments into public cloud infrastructure while preserving existing engineering workflows.

## Business Challenges

* Limited on-premises compute capacity
* Long procurement cycles
* Rising infrastructure costs
* Inefficient resource utilization
* Growing semiconductor design complexity
* Increased verification and simulation workloads

## Proposed Solution

The platform provides:

* Hybrid HPC architecture
* Cloud bursting capability
* Shared storage integration
* Scheduler integration (Slurm and LSF)
* License-aware scheduling
* Operational analytics
* AI-powered optimization

## Business Benefits

### Operational Benefits

* Reduced job wait times
* Faster simulation turnaround
* Improved utilization

### Financial Benefits

* Reduced capital expenditure
* Consumption-based scaling
* Improved cost efficiency

### Strategic Benefits

* Multi-cloud readiness
* Future AI adoption
* Platform modernization

## Roadmap

## Conclusion

The platform enables semiconductor organizations to achieve scalable, secure, and cost-efficient engineering infrastructure while creating a foundation for AI-driven operations.
